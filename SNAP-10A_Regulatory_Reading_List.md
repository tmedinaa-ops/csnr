# SNAP-10A / SNAPSHOT — Regulatory & Safety Document Reading List

Compiled for the NSPM-20 / nuclear electric propulsion policy analysis. Sources are the SNAP-10A flight papers (SAE 650793; *Nucl. Eng. Des.* 5, 1967) and the *Annotated Bibliography, SNAP 10A Program* (NAA-SR-MEMO-12023, 1966).

## How to read this list

- Each entry gives the **report number** (the authoritative locator), title, author, and date.
- Live OSTI links are included where a record was confirmed. Where no link appears, the report is retrievable on [osti.gov](https://www.osti.gov) or [DTIC](https://discover.dtic.mil) by searching the report number.
- Report numbers were transcribed from a 1966 OCR scan — **verify digits against OSTI before citing.**

## 1. Governing authority at the time (1965)

There was no single codified launch rule in 1965. The first formalized federal launch-approval procedure was **PD/NSC-25 (1977)**; before that, approval was statutory + *ad hoc*. SNAP-10A was regulated by:

- **Atomic Energy Act of 1954** — AEC's statutory responsibility for the nuclear material.
- **Ad hoc interagency nuclear safety review** — case-by-case panel; the pre-1977 predecessor to the INSRP later codified in PD/NSC-25.
- **Launch range safety** — USAF and U.S. Navy Pacific Missile Range (launch from Vandenberg).
- **AEC Aerospace Nuclear Safety Program** — Atomics International (safety analysis) + Sandia (independent review and destructive testing).

Regulatory genealogy for the modern comparison: **Atomic Energy Act 1954 → ad hoc panels (NSAM era, 1961–63) → PD/NSC-25 (1977, standing INSRP) → NSPM-20 (2019, tiering + risk metrics).** SNAP-10A sits at the loosest, least formalized end of this line.

## 2. The mission safeguards / approval submissions

These are the documents the launch approval was actually based on — the closest 1965 analog to an NSPM-20 safety analysis.

- **NAA-SR-10022 (Rev)** — *Final SNAPSHOT Safeguards Report* — Staff, 20 Mar 1965 (+ Addendum 1). Complete launch-to-reentry safety case in three phases: (a) launch to orbit injection, (b) orbit injection to startup command, (c) startup to reentry. **The capstone regulatory document.**
- **NAA-SR-6684** — *Preliminary SNAP 10A–SNAPSHOT Safety Report* — M. Huntsinger, 26 Feb 1962. Joint Lockheed + Atomics International; written explicitly "for use by safety review and approval agencies within the U.S. AEC, the U.S. Air Force, and the U.S. Navy Pacific Missile Range."
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-10246** — *Summary Report SNAPSHOT Safety* — Staff, 15 Aug 1964.
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-7399** — *SNAPSHOT Safety Program Plan* — M. F. Huntsinger, 20 Jun 1962. → [OSTI 966134](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/966134)
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-6224** — *SNAPSHOT Nuclear Flight Safety Program* — Staff, 31 Mar 1961 (the 1961 preliminary plan). → [OSTI 4648974](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4648974)
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-8147** — *SNAP 10A Power System Shipment Safeguards Report* — R. S. Hart, 18 Dec 1963.
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-8685** — *SNAP Prelaunch Test Facility Safeguards Report* — B. F. Ureda, 31 Dec 1963.
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-7286** — *Acceptance Test Facility Safeguards Report* — W. H. Heneveld, 17 Apr 1963.

## 3. Aerospace-safety, reentry & criticality basis

The technical analyses and tests underpinning the safety case — and the documents that addressed the 1961 open problems.

- **NAA-SR-8462** — *Aerospace Safety Results of Special Interest to SNAP 10A Flight Tests* — K. E. Buttrey, 15 Jul 1963. Covers water-immersion reactivity, nuclear-accident behavior, reentry breakup/ablation, fission-product release. The single best summary of how the gaps were attacked.
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-8972** — *SNAP 10A Launch and Reentry Hazards Due to Steady-State Operation* — R. E. Alexander, 1 Sep 1964. Analyzes the residual case where reentry/impact forces fail to render the core subcritical. → [OSTI 4195957](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4195957)
- **NAA-SR-5482** — *Evaluation of Radiological Hazards Due to Reentry of SNAP APU* — F. D. Anderson, 15 Jan 1961.
- **NAA-SR-11103** — *Disintegration and Dispersion of the SNAP 10A Reactor Upon Return from Satellite Orbit* — R. D. Elliott, 1 Sep 1965. Concludes the reactor disperses above ~237,000 ft.
- **NAA-SR-8303** — *Aerospace Safety Re-entry Analytical and Experimental Program, SNAP 2 and 10A (Interim Report)* — R. D. Elliott, 1963. → [OSTI 4657830](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4657830)
- **TID-18880** — *Experiments with Water-Reflected, Undermoderated Zirconium Hydride Critical Assemblies* — L. I. Moss, 1963. The dedicated water-submersion criticality experiments.
- **SNAPTRAN destructive-excursion tests** — deliberate reactor destruction to bound the worst-case excursion:
  - IDO-16825 — *Safety Analysis Report, SNAPTRAN 2/10A-1 Safety Tests* (Bentzen & DeHerrin, 1963)
  - IDO-17019 / IDO-17071 — *SNAPTRAN 2/10A-3 Destructive Test Results* / *Post-Destructive Test Examination* (Phillips Petroleum, 1964–66)
  - IDO-17038 — *Radiological Aspects of SNAPTRAN 2/10A-5 Destructive Tests* (Cordes et al., 1964)
  - *Radiological Aspects of the SNAPTRAN-2 Destructive Test* → [OSTI 4351114](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4351114)

## 4. Dose criteria & population risk — most useful for the old-vs-new comparison

- **NAA-SR-11761** — *Radiological Aspects of Reentry Burnup of SNAP Reactors* — C. A. Willis, 1965/66. Works from the **ICRP population-dose limit of 2 rem / 30 yr** and recommends **0.25 rem / 30 yr** as allowable from SNAP applications. **Set this against NSPM-20's 25 rem TED at ≥1-in-1,000,000 probability** — the metric shifted from a deterministic population-dose limit to a probabilistic individual-dose-risk tier. This is the spine of a modern analysis.
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-11148** — *An Analysis of the World Population Density Distribution* — Kenfield & McAdams, 16 Jul 1965. Population-at-risk methodology; ancestor of today's expected-casualty calculations.

## 5. Agency criteria & policy papers

- **AEC Aerospace Safety Programs and Philosophy** — U.S. Atomic Energy Commission. The AEC's own statement of its aerospace-nuclear-safety approach. → [OSTI 4115550](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4115550)
- **AFSWC-TDR-62-83** — *Flight Test Criteria for Reentering SNAP System* — Air Force Special Weapons Center, Nov 1962. Actual government criteria, not contractor analysis.
- **TID-20079** — *An Evaluation of Systems for Nuclear Auxiliary Power* — U.S. AEC, Jan 1964.
- **SC-RR-64-501** — *RFD-1: Final SNAP 10A Safety Flight Test Plan* — Erickson et al. (Sandia), Mar 1963. The alternate "burn-up on reentry" disposal philosophy — the counterfactual to the high-orbit approach SNAPSHOT used. → [OSTI 4072008](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4072008)
- **ARS Paper 1764-61** — *Hazards Associated with Nuclear Power Reactors in Space Applications* — P. G. Lafyatis. Early open-literature policy overview.
- **NAA-SR-MEMO-8215, Part 7** — *SNAP 10A Flight Nuclear Safety* — R. G. Courson, 1 May 1963. Notable for deliberately generalizing its conclusions to "any reactor nuclear-power system."

## 1961 open problems → how (and whether) they were resolved

| 1961 gap (NAA-SR-MEMO-6224) | What addressed it | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Water-submersion criticality | TID-18880 water-reflected ZrH experiments; NAA-SR-8462 immersion tests | Worked experimentally; fed geometry/poison design — **partially closed** |
| Inherent subcriticality / power excursion | SNAPTRAN destructive tests (IDO-16825/17019/17071/17038) | Worst case bounded empirically — **bounded, not eliminated** |
| Reentry burnup unverified | NAA-SR-9779 (wind tunnel), NAA-SR-11103 (dispersal >237,000 ft) | Verified analytically/experimentally — but **not used for flight** (high-orbit storage chosen instead) |
| Residual case: subcriticality fails on impact/reentry | NAA-SR-MEMO-8972 | Consequence bounded; **acknowledged unresolved** |
| End-of-life controlled disposal | (none) — relied on high parking orbit | **Never solved; still open today** (the core NSPM-20 gap) |
| Spurious safing actuation | (design requirement only) | **Confirmed as a failure mode by the 1965 flight** (command decoder sent spurious shutdown) |
| Approval-agency framework undefined | Resolved into the §2 safeguards-report chain to AEC/USAF/Navy PMR | Closed procedurally |

**Through-line for the thesis:** SNAP-10A's safety program closed the *get-it-safely-to-orbit* problems and bounded the accident cases, but never solved *controlled end-of-life disposal* — exactly the requirement NSPM-20 later made explicit. The dose standard also shifted from ICRP population dose (2 rem/30 yr) to NSPM-20's probabilistic 25 rem / 1-in-10⁶.
